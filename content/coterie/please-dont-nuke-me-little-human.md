i don't think my skin will melt like your little human doll.  
i won't jolt and spark like metal forks.  
i'll just sit, and watch, and flick my tongue.  

don't place me on your human scale,  
in a bowl on your kitchen countertop,  
just to count each ounce i'm worth.  

i watched you nuke a chicken's egg,   
until it froze into sterile rubber,  
its coffin clicking like gravel-logged wheels.  

i'm not fated for the unknowable agonies  
behind that panel of buttons i've watched  
encode dark rituals incomprehensible.  

besides, you don't want to clean lizard-eye juice,  
so please don't nuke me, little human.

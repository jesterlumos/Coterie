you can major in business and learn 
to herd rows of numbers into well-behaved 
flocks and graphs and collected figures 
so they look good to the board,
and call yourself a silent majority.

nursing is a major draw,
offering compassion as a route
through major arteries and ANP flashcards
to the pot of rejected claims at the end
of a corporate rainbow.

maybe you’ll major in something rhythmic;
learn to move your hips and click 
your heels like a metronome, or pluck 
a major scale on gut strings suspended 
over a dead cavity of resonance.

ask yourself big questions:
“can i major in astronomy and rip
the arrows of 1,000 hunters from ursa major?” 
“can i precess earth’s major axis and etch 
spirograms into the tar of the cosmos?”

ask, “can i major in majoring, and live 
in the walls and beating womb 
of a college campus?” “can i major in sumi-e 
ink pouring from the brink of a brush, 
reminding us to twirl like the winds?”

or just let the poetry take you, 
and study what makes you ask.
